Everything you need to get into ketosis comfortably — what to expect, how to dodge the keto flu, and a cheat sheet you can stick on the fridge.

The first two weeks are the whole game. Your body is switching from running on sugar to running on fat — and most people who quit keto quit in week one, almost always for reasons that are completely preventable. Here's your map.

What to expect
01 The 14-day adaptation curve
DAYS 1–3Switching over

Burning through stored sugar

Your glycogen empties and you shed water weight fast — often 2–4 lb. The scale flatters you now; that's water, not fat. Drink more than feels normal.

DAYS 4–7The flu window

Where it can get rough

Headaches, fatigue, or irritability can show up. This is the "keto flu" — and it's an electrolyte problem, not a sign keto is failing you. Page 2 prevents it.

DAYS 8–14Fat-adapted

The lights come back on

Energy stabilizes, cravings fade, and hunger quiets down. Many people report the clearest focus they've had in years right around here.

The golden rule
02 Stay under your carb ceiling
Your number is 25g net carbs per day Net carbs = total carbs − fiber − sugar alcohols. Stay under this and ketosis takes care of itself. The cheat sheet on page 3 shows exactly which foods fit — keep it somewhere you'll see it.

Keto-flu prevention
03 The three minerals that decide your week
As insulin drops, your kidneys flush sodium — and potassium and magnesium follow. Replace all three from day one and the keto flu mostly disappears before it starts.
Na
Sodium
3,000–5,000 mg/day
Salt your food generously, sip broth daily, add a pinch to water. This is the single biggest lever.
K
Potassium
~3,500 mg/day
Avocado, leafy greens, salmon, mushrooms. A potassium-chloride "lite salt" helps fill the gap.
Mg
Magnesium
300–400 mg/day
Hardest to get from food. A glycinate or citrate supplement at night also helps sleep and cramps.

Your first week, simplified
06 Five rules that carry you
  • Salt everything. The keto flu is almost always a sodium problem. A cup of broth a day is cheap insurance.
  • Eat fat to fullness, don't fear it. Hunger is your gauge — you don't need to count every gram in week one.
  • Keep carbs under 25g. Lean on the green column. When in doubt, protein + fat + greens.
  • Drink more water than feels normal. You're flushing a lot of it early on.
  • Ignore the scale after day 3. Early drops are water. Real fat loss shows up over weeks, not days.
You've got this, TEST999 When week-one gets hard, it's almost never willpower — it's electrolytes. Salt, hydrate, sleep, and let the cravings pass. By day 10 most people wonder what the fuss was about.
