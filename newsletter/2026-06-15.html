Dial-in of the week

Your Keto Bloodwork Came Back 'Bad.' Here's What's Actually Going On.

So your doctor ran a lipid panel and now you're staring at a number that's highlighted in red. Before you spiral, let's talk about what keto actually does to your blood markers, because it's more nuanced than most standard lab reports let on.

Total cholesterol often goes up. That's not the whole story. On keto, a lot of people see their total cholesterol rise in the first few months. The number that matters more is the ratio of your HDL (the protective kind) to your triglycerides. Keto tends to raise HDL and drop triglycerides, which is generally considered a favorable shift by researchers who study cardiometabolic health. A common pattern people report is triglycerides dropping well below 150 mg/dL while HDL climbs above 50.

LDL is more complicated than one number. Standard lipid panels report total LDL, but researchers increasingly look at LDL particle size. Small, dense LDL particles are considered more problematic than large, fluffy ones. Keto tends to shift people toward larger LDL particles. Your standard panel won't show this, so it's worth asking your doctor about an NMR lipoprofile if you want a fuller picture. That said, always work with your doctor on interpreting your specific results, not a newsletter.

Some markers look weird in the short term. Fasting glucose can tick up slightly in early keto, even as insulin sensitivity improves. This is sometimes called physiological insulin resistance, where your body is prioritizing glucose for the tissues that need it. It doesn't mean keto is hurting you, but it does mean the timing of your bloodwork matters. A test done in week three of keto tells a very different story than one done after six months of fat adaptation.

What to actually say to your doctor. Bring context. Tell them when you started keto, what your diet looks like roughly, and ask for a full metabolic panel including fasting insulin and hsCRP if possible. A doctor who understands low-carb diets will want that context. One who doesn't may just see the flagged LDL number and default to a script.

If you're trying to make sure your macros are dialed in before your next panel, the KetoDial calculator is a good place to start. Getting protein and fat in the right range for your body matters, and the defaults most people use online are often off. Sarah's full article on bloodwork goes much deeper on all of this, including the specific numbers worth tracking over time.

 
Cook this week

Grilled Chicken Thighs with Herb Butter and Charred Lemon

Serves: 2  |  Prep + Cook: 25 min  |  Macros (per serving): 520 kcal / 36g fat / 44g protein / 2g net carbs

This is the recipe to bring to every BBQ this summer, because it looks impressive, takes 25 minutes, and nobody will ask you a single diet question.

What you need:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • 1 lemon, halved

  1. Mix the softened butter with parsley, thyme, and minced garlic. Stir until combined, then set aside at room temperature.
  2. Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. This is the step most people skip, and it's the reason your skin won't crisp. Don't skip it.
  3. Rub the thighs all over with avocado oil, then season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
  4. Heat your grill to medium-high. Place thighs skin-side down and don't touch them for 7 minutes. You want a real sear, not steaming.
  5. Flip and cook another 12 to 14 minutes until the internal temperature hits 165°F. Place the lemon halves cut-side down on the grill during the last 5 minutes.
  6. Pull the chicken off the heat and immediately top each thigh with a generous spoonful of herb butter. Let it rest 3 minutes before serving.
  7. Squeeze the grilled lemon over everything right before eating. The heat mellows the bitterness and you get a sweeter, deeper citrus flavor than raw lemon.

Why it works for keto: Bone-in, skin-on thighs are one of the best cuts for keto because the fat content is naturally higher than breast meat, the skin crisps up into something genuinely satisfying, and they're significantly cheaper per pound. Avocado oil handles the heat well without breaking down, and the herb butter adds fat and flavor right at the end when it can actually coat the meat properly. If you're cooking for a BBQ crowd, this scales up easily and keeps well under foil for 20 to 30 minutes.

 
This week's tiny win
This week at the store, pick up avocado oil spray instead of your regular cooking spray. Most standard sprays use seed oils with low smoke points that break down fast over high heat. Avocado oil handles temperatures up to around 500°F, so it's a straightforward swap that makes your everyday cooking cleaner without any extra effort.
