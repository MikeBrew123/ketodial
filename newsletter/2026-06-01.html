Dial-in of the week

Why Protein Is the Macro Most People Get Wrong on Keto

Here's the mistake I see constantly: someone starts keto, cuts carbs way down (great), bumps up fat (good), and barely thinks about protein. Then three months later they've lost weight but look soft and feel weak. That's not a keto problem. That's a protein problem.

Your body needs protein to preserve lean mass while you're burning fat. Without enough, your body breaks down muscle for the amino acids it needs. The research on protein-sparing is clear: when you're in a caloric deficit, higher protein intake is what protects the muscle you already have.

The number to remember: 1.5 to 2g of protein per kilogram of lean body mass. Not total body weight. Lean mass. For most people, that's significantly more protein than they're currently eating.

Don't want to do that math yourself? That's literally what the KetoDial calculator is built for. Plug in your stats and it dials in your protein target automatically, scaled to your lean mass.

Get protein right and everything else on keto gets easier.

 
Cook this week

Garlic-Butter Ribeye with Charred Asparagus

Serves: 1  |  Prep + Cook: 20 min  |  Macros: 620 kcal / 47g fat / 43g protein / 5g net carbs

This one's a weeknight winner. Twenty minutes, one pan, and you're eating like a king while staying right in your macros.

What you need:
8 oz ribeye steak, 1 tbsp butter, 2 cloves garlic (smashed), 6-8 asparagus spears (trimmed), salt and pepper, splash of avocado oil

  1. Pat the ribeye dry, season both sides generously with salt and pepper, and let it sit at room temp for 10 minutes while you prep everything else.
  2. Heat avocado oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat until it just starts to smoke, then sear the ribeye for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare.
  3. In the last minute of cooking, drop the butter and smashed garlic into the pan and baste the steak by spooning that melted garlic butter over the top.
  4. Pull the steak out to rest, toss the asparagus into the same pan with all that leftover garlic butter, and char them for 2-3 minutes, rolling them once.
  5. Slice the ribeye against the grain, plate it next to the asparagus, and spoon any remaining pan butter over the top.

Why it works for keto: High fat from the ribeye and butter keeps you satisfied for hours. Adequate protein supports muscle without overdoing it. And the asparagus adds fiber and nutrients for just 5g net carbs. This is exactly the kind of meal the KetoDial calculator builds for you.

 
What we're hearing

Three things keep coming up in every keto group we follow, and they're all connected.

First, the net carbs vs. total carbs confusion. People are second-guessing every label and it's stalling them before they even start. Second, "keto flu" is still the number one reason people quit in week one. Most of the time it's an electrolyte problem, not a diet problem. We wrote a whole guide on that.

And third, the one nobody warns you about: social pressure. Turns out explaining your food choices at a dinner table is harder than skipping the bread. If that sounds familiar, you're not alone. We've got you covered on all three.
